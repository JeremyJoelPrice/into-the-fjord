---
title: Disease
---

It's fairly common to encounter diseased creatures or people. You become
infected if you fail a Might roll after being exposed to a disease.  
Become exposed by:

- a diseased creature attacking you and dealing Might damage
- a trap purposely designed to infect victims
- environmental hazards, insects, or ingesting spoiled food

In such a martial society there are actually social advantages to being
diseased:

- you are seen as too weak to matter; secrets are spoken aloud in front of you
- able to slip into restricted places
- people are kinder to you, especially exiles, elves, and other outsiders

## Common Diseases

Each day when you wake, roll d6 and suffer the corresponding effect.

### Bone-sickness

Cure: Ingest powdered troll bones  
Effects (d6):

1. Reduce total Might by d4
2. Gain Fatigue
3. Red patches on hands
4. Sore limbs
5. Roll d4: that effect recovers for good
6. Disease ends, all effects recover

### Blood-sickness

Cure: Bathe in near-boiling water  
Effects (d6):

1. Reduce total Grace by d6
2. Extreme vertigo when moving up or down e.g. ladders
3. Can't eat without being sick
4. Lose sense of smell
5. Roll d4: that effect recovers for good
6. Disease ends, all effects recover

### Mind-sickness

Cure: Rub freshly pressed berries in your ears  
Effects (d6):

1. Reduce total Will by d4
2. Require a stick to walk
3. Blue eyelids
4. Swollen neck
5. Ghosts & elves are visible to you
6. Disease ends, all effects recover

### Flesh-sickness

_A lump grows on your back._  
Cure: Cover an axe in both fire and ice, then use it to cut off the lump  
Effects (d6):

1. Lump consumes a gear slot
2. Lump consumes a gear slot
3. Lump sprouts a mushroom
4. Lump moves to another part of your back
5. Lump shrinks, freeing a gear slot.
6. Lump shrivels away for good.
