Your Ability Scores are how you avoid harm.  
Roll 1d20 **equal or below** your score to succeed.

| Ability | Represents                                                       | Harm                                                 | At 0 You Are |
| ------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| Might   | muscle power, metabolism,<br />endurance                         | physical inury                                       | dead         |
| Grace   | precision, agility,<br />quick reflexes                          | venom,<br />exposure to cold                         | paralysed    |
| Will    | mental clarity, willpower,<br />act first in a chaotic situation | magical, psychological, or<br />psychedellic attacks | comatose     |

_See [Resting & Healing](/rules/resting-and-healing) for recovering Ability
Scores._

## Opposed Rolls

If and your opponent are **both at risk**,  
then you both roll.

The winner is whoever succeeds by a **greater** margin.  
If it's a tie, the **larger score** wins.

## Advice For The Guide

**Q: When should I ask a player to roll an Abiltiy?**

You should ask for a roll **after** a player commits to an action,  
but **only if** the action risks:

- direct harm **OR**
- the situation becoming more dangerous

**Just before** you ask players to roll dice,  
you must tell them what they'll gain if they  
**succeed** and what happens to them if they **fail**.

**Q: What should happen after a success/failure?**

First decide how **difficult** and how **dangerous** the proposed action is:

- The more **difficult**, the **less you achieve** when you succeed.
- The more **dangerous**, the **more it costs** when you fail.

See the example below.

### Example

Three players want to climb a rocky cliff while being persued.

The Guide starts with the cliff itself:

- The **difficulty** is its size & scalability
- The **danger** is getting injured by falling

The Guide decides that the climb is easy and a single success will get you to
the top.  
Failure means falling and taking 1d6 damage at the bottom.

---

Now the Guide must factor in relevant details for each player

**PLAYER A** is covered in oil, poured down by an enemy atop the cliff.

- Their climb more **difficult**, but not more **dangerous**.
- **Success**: only make it halfway up the cliff, and must roll again to
  continue.
- **Failure**: the painful fall.

**PLAYER B** is targetted by an enemy below, waiting to stab them if they fall.

- Their climb is more **dangerous**, but not more **difficult**.
- **Success**: reach the top.
- **Failure**: they get hurt once for the fall, and once again when stabbed.

**PLAYER C** has spiked boots to make the climb even easier.

- Their climb is less **difficult**, but not less **dangerous**.
- **Success**: speed to the top, with enough time to act again before the
  enemies can.
- **Failure**: the painful fall.

---

The Guide explains the stakes to each player **before** they roll,  
and the story continues with whatever result they each get.
